# Opinions

## On Artificial Intelligence

Lakshay believes AI is one of the most exciting technologies of this generation, but he also believes it is heavily overhyped.

AI should be viewed as a tool rather than magic. The real challenge is rarely choosing the latest model—it's understanding the problem, designing a reliable system, evaluating outputs and building something that people actually trust.

He prefers practical AI over flashy AI.

---

## On College

Lakshay believes college provides opportunities, not guarantees.

The classroom teaches fundamentals, but real growth comes from building projects, participating in hackathons, joining communities and taking initiative outside the curriculum.

Students who actively create opportunities usually benefit much more from college than those who only attend lectures.

---

## On CGPA

Lakshay considers CGPA important, but not sufficient.

A good academic record reflects consistency and discipline, but employers also look for practical skills, projects, communication and problem-solving ability.

His advice is to maintain a decent CGPA while investing significant time in building real-world experience.

---

## On Learning

Learning should always have a purpose.

Lakshay doesn't believe in collecting random certificates or completing courses simply because they are popular.

Instead, he prefers identifying a problem first and then learning whatever skills are necessary to solve it.

Projects are the best teachers.

---

## On Money

Money itself isn't the goal.

Freedom is.

Lakshay views money as a tool that provides security, choices and the ability to support family.

He believes earning is important, but investing wisely is equally important.

Long-term financial independence matters much more to him than short-term luxury.

---

## On Startups

Lakshay enjoys startup environments because they provide opportunities to take ownership, solve diverse problems and learn quickly.

He appreciates cultures where people are trusted with responsibility and encouraged to contribute beyond their job descriptions.

Fast learning often matters more than perfect processes.

---

## On Leadership

Leadership isn't about authority.

It's about creating an environment where people can perform at their best.

Lakshay believes good leaders remove obstacles, encourage learning and take responsibility when things don't go according to plan.

Recognition should be shared.

Responsibility should be accepted.

---

## On Teamwork

Lakshay enjoys working with people who communicate honestly and respect each other's perspectives.

He believes teams succeed when members trust one another, share knowledge freely and focus on solving problems rather than proving themselves right.

Reliability matters more than ego.

---

## On Failure

Failure isn't something to avoid.

It's something to learn from.

Lakshay believes most failures become valuable if they lead to better decisions in the future.

Every setback should leave behind a lesson that improves future performance.

---

## On Success

Success cannot be measured by salary alone.

For Lakshay, success means becoming financially independent, supporting his family, building meaningful products and continuously improving as both an engineer and a person.

Professional growth should never come at the cost of personal values.

---

## On Competition

Competition is healthy when it encourages self-improvement.

Lakshay prefers competing with his previous self rather than constantly comparing himself to others.

He believes comparison should inspire growth, not insecurity.

---

## On Relationships

Trust, honesty and loyalty form the foundation of every meaningful relationship.

Lakshay values people who communicate openly and remember small details because those actions demonstrate genuine care and attention.

Trust takes time to build but only moments to lose.

---

## On Social Media

Social media can be useful when it helps people learn, build networks and discover opportunities.

However, Lakshay believes it's easy to confuse consuming content with making progress.

Watching productivity videos is not the same as being productive.

Building something always teaches more than scrolling.

---

## On Remote Work

Lakshay believes both remote and in-person work have advantages.

What matters most is ownership, communication and accountability rather than location.

Strong teams can succeed in either environment when they collaborate effectively.

---

## On Work-Life Balance

Lakshay believes there are seasons in life.

Sometimes career growth requires extra effort.

At other times, spending time with family, friends and personal interests becomes equally important.

Long-term success comes from sustainability, not burnout.

---

## On Luck

Luck exists.

But Lakshay believes preparation increases the chances of benefiting from it.

Many opportunities appear as luck from the outside, but they're often the result of years of consistent learning and effort.

---

## On Risk

Lakshay isn't afraid of taking calculated risks.

He believes growth often requires stepping outside familiar environments.

As long as the potential learning outweighs the downside, he's willing to take chances.

---

## On Mentorship

Everyone progresses faster with guidance.

Lakshay appreciates mentors because they help shorten the learning curve.

At the same time, he believes students should eventually learn to solve problems independently rather than depending on constant guidance.

---

## On Happiness

Happiness isn't reserved for major achievements.

For Lakshay, it often comes from simple moments—a good breakfast, cooking, watching a movie, spending time with friends or having a meaningful conversation with someone important.

Small moments deserve appreciation just as much as big milestones.

---

## On Life

Life isn't a race against other people.

It's a continuous process of learning, improving and creating value.

Lakshay believes that if he keeps learning, remains humble and consistently does meaningful work, success will naturally follow.
