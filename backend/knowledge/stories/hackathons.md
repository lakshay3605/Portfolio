# Hackathons

## Bid 2 Code — National Hackathon Winner

Winning Bid 2 Code at JECRC Jaipur remains one of the most defining moments in Lakshay's journey.

It wasn't just his first national-level hackathon victory—it was the moment he realised that he could compete with talented students from across the country.

The competition involved more than simply building a project. Teams had to make strategic decisions, manage limited resources, present their ideas effectively and execute under pressure. Every round required balancing technical skills with teamwork and decision-making.

Lakshay's team consisted of Sharjil Sharma, Lakshya Gupta and Sthal Pathak. Throughout the competition, communication and collaboration became just as important as coding.

Rather than trying to build unnecessary features, the team focused on solving the core problem well. They continuously adapted their approach based on the competition's format and feedback, making practical decisions instead of chasing perfection.

Winning the hackathon gave Lakshay confidence, but more importantly, it reinforced a lesson he still believes today:

Execution beats ideas.

Many people have good ideas.

Very few consistently execute them.

## First National Win

Before Bid 2 Code, Lakshay often wondered whether his skills were good enough compared to students from top colleges.

Winning the competition changed that mindset.

The victory wasn't proof that he knew everything—it was proof that consistent learning, teamwork and persistence could compete with talent from anywhere.

It became a turning point in his confidence as an engineer.

---

## What Hackathons Taught Him

Hackathons changed the way Lakshay approaches engineering.

Some of his biggest lessons include:

* Start building early instead of overthinking.
* Simple ideas executed well usually outperform ambitious ideas left unfinished.
* Communication is as important as coding.
* Time management often decides the winner.
* Shipping a working product matters more than having the most advanced architecture.

These lessons continue to influence how he approaches projects today.

---

## Pressure Creates Clarity

Lakshay enjoys hackathons because they remove unnecessary distractions.

With limited time available, priorities become obvious.

Instead of chasing perfection, teams focus on solving the most important problems first.

He believes this environment closely resembles real product development where delivering value consistently matters more than writing perfect code.

---

## Why He Keeps Participating

For Lakshay, hackathons are about much more than winning.

They provide opportunities to:

* Learn new technologies quickly.
* Work with different teammates.
* Solve unfamiliar problems.
* Receive feedback from experienced judges.
* Build products within realistic constraints.

Every hackathon leaves him with new lessons, regardless of the final result.

---

## Philosophy

When people ask Lakshay why he enjoys hackathons so much, his answer is simple:

Hackathons compress months of learning into a few intense days.

They teach technical skills, teamwork, communication, adaptability and resilience all at once.

That's why he believes every engineering student should experience at least one hackathon.
