# Internship

## Joining KVGAI

Joining KVGAI marked the transition from building projects primarily for learning to working on AI systems with real users and real expectations.

Until then, most of Lakshay's experience came from hackathons, personal projects and community work. The internship introduced him to collaborative development, structured workflows and the responsibility that comes with building AI products used by others.

It reinforced an important lesson:

Writing code is only one part of engineering. Building reliable products requires understanding users, testing ideas and continuously improving them.

---

## Working with Large Language Models

At KVGAI, Lakshay worked extensively with Large Language Models, prompt engineering and evaluation workflows.

Rather than thinking of prompts as simple inputs, he learned to view them as part of a complete product experience.

Good AI systems aren't created by writing one perfect prompt.

They're built through continuous experimentation, evaluation, iteration and improvement.

This experience strengthened his appreciation for systematic thinking over trial and error.

---

## Product Thinking

One of the biggest changes in Lakshay's mindset during the internship was moving from feature thinking to product thinking.

Instead of asking,

"What feature should I build?"

he started asking,

"What problem is the user actually trying to solve?"

That small shift changed the way he approached engineering.

Every technical decision became connected to user experience, reliability and long-term maintainability.

---

## Learning Through Ownership

One thing Lakshay appreciated about the internship was the opportunity to take ownership.

Whenever he worked on a feature or workflow, he naturally wanted to understand how it fit into the larger product instead of focusing only on his assigned task.

He enjoys environments where engineers are trusted to think beyond implementation and contribute ideas that improve the final product.

---

## Collaboration

Working with experienced engineers taught Lakshay that software development is fundamentally a team activity.

Clear communication, asking thoughtful questions and sharing ideas often save more time than trying to solve everything alone.

The internship reinforced the importance of collaboration, documentation and knowledge sharing.

---

## Continuous Learning

The AI ecosystem changes rapidly.

Rather than trying to learn everything, Lakshay focused on building strong fundamentals while staying curious about new models, tools and workflows.

The internship strengthened his belief that learning never stops.

Every project became an opportunity to improve both technical skills and engineering judgment.

---

## Building for Users

Perhaps the biggest lesson Lakshay took away from the internship is that users don't care how advanced the technology is.

They care whether the product works.

Whether the answer is accurate.

Whether the interface is simple.

Whether they can trust it.

That perspective continues to influence every project he builds.

---

## Looking Ahead

The internship gave Lakshay confidence that he wants to continue building AI products professionally.

It also made him realize that his interests extend beyond writing models.

He enjoys understanding products, collaborating with teams, improving user experiences and taking ownership from idea to deployment.

That combination of engineering, product thinking and continuous learning is the direction he hopes to continue throughout his career.

---

## Key Lessons

If Lakshay had to summarize his internship in a few lessons, they would be:

* Build for users, not for demos.
* Ownership creates better engineers.
* AI products require continuous evaluation and iteration.
* Communication is as valuable as technical ability.
* Curiosity and consistency matter more than knowing everything.
* Shipping a useful product is more meaningful than building a technically impressive prototype that nobody uses.

These lessons continue to shape how he approaches engineering today.
