# College

## Choosing Computer Science

Initially, Lakshay had considered pursuing Electrical Engineering. As he explored different fields and technologies, he realized he was far more interested in building software, solving problems through technology and creating products that people could actually use.

Looking back, choosing Computer Science became one of the best decisions of his academic journey.

---

## Discovering AI

During the early years of college, AI was simply another interesting field.

As Lakshay started experimenting with APIs, machine learning models and real-world projects, he realized that AI wasn't just about research papers or algorithms—it was a way to build products that could genuinely help people.

That realization shifted his focus from simply learning AI to building AI-powered products.

---

## Learning Beyond the Classroom

One lesson Lakshay learned early in college was that lectures alone weren't enough.

Most of his growth came from:

* Building projects
* Participating in hackathons
* Learning from documentation
* Exploring new technologies
* Talking with seniors and mentors
* Solving real-world problems

College provided the environment, but curiosity determined how much he learned from it.

---

## The DSA Lesson

One mistake Lakshay openly talks about is buying a DSA course simply because everyone else was doing the same.

At that time, he hadn't asked himself why he wanted to learn it.

Eventually, he realized that blindly following trends wasn't helping him grow.

That experience completely changed his learning philosophy.

Today, he always begins with the problem he wants to solve and then learns whatever skills are required.

---

## Community Leadership

College gave Lakshay opportunities beyond academics.

He took leadership roles in technical communities because he enjoyed helping students build confidence through practical experience.

Leading THINK AI and HackSphere allowed him to organize workshops, technical sessions, hackathons and collaborative events that encouraged students to build rather than just consume content.

These experiences strengthened not only his leadership skills but also his communication, planning and ability to work with diverse teams.

---

## Building Confidence

College wasn't only about learning technology.

It was also about proving to himself that he was capable.

Winning hackathons, securing internships and leading technical communities gradually replaced self-doubt with confidence.

Those achievements didn't make him believe he knew everything.

Instead, they reminded him that consistent effort eventually produces meaningful results.

---

## Friendships

One of Lakshay's favorite parts of college has been the people.

Some of his best memories have nothing to do with classrooms.

They're the random conversations after lectures, team meetings before hackathons, shared meals, inside jokes and long discussions about careers, startups and the future.

He believes the people you meet in college often shape your journey just as much as the subjects you study.

---

## Beyond Academics

Lakshay believes college should never be limited to earning a degree.

It should be a place to experiment, fail, build, compete, lead and discover what genuinely excites you.

The classroom teaches concepts.

Experience teaches confidence.

Both are important, but together they create much stronger engineers.

---

## Advice to College Students

If Lakshay could give one piece of advice to students entering college, it would be this:

Don't spend all four years waiting for opportunities to come to you.

Join technical communities.

Build projects.

Participate in hackathons.

Talk to seniors.

Take responsibility.

Explore different fields during your first year, but once you discover what genuinely interests you, stop chasing everything else and go deep.

Those experiences will shape your career far more than simply attending lectures.

---

## Looking Back

When Lakshay looks back at his college journey, he doesn't first think about grades.

He thinks about the people he met, the communities he helped build, the hackathons he participated in, the internship he earned and the confidence he gained through consistent effort.

College became much more than an education.

It became the place where he transformed from a curious student into an engineer who enjoys building products, leading teams and continuously learning.
