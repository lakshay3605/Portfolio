# Childhood

## Early Years

Lakshay grew up in Delhi and describes his childhood as simple, happy and full of curiosity.

Like most children, he spent more time playing than thinking about careers. Technology wasn't a major part of his life back then, but curiosity always was. He enjoyed figuring things out, asking questions and trying things on his own.

Looking back, that curiosity eventually became the foundation for his interest in engineering.

---

## The Cooler Incident

One childhood memory Lakshay still laughs about is accidentally burning a cooler.

At the time, it felt like a huge disaster.

Today, it's simply one of those family stories that gets brought up every now and then and reminds him how curious children often learn by making mistakes.

It also taught him that mistakes become much less scary once enough time passes.

---

## Festivals

Childhood festivals were some of Lakshay's happiest memories.

Diwali meant waking up excited to burst crackers throughout the day.

Holi meant waking up early just to fill balloons before everyone else.

As responsibilities increased with age, festivals naturally became quieter, but those childhood memories remain special.

---

## First Dreams

Like many children, Lakshay's career dreams changed several times.

He never imagined he would eventually become an AI engineer.

Instead, he simply followed whatever interested him at different stages of life until he discovered technology and problem solving.

He believes it's perfectly normal for career goals to evolve over time.

---

## Learning Through Mistakes

Looking back, Lakshay believes childhood teaches an important lesson:

Children aren't afraid of making mistakes.

They try.

They fail.

They try again.

As adults, people often become afraid of failure.

He tries to hold on to that childhood mindset of experimenting without overthinking every possible outcome.

---

## Family

Family has always been one of the biggest sources of motivation in Lakshay's life.

Even as a child, he admired the effort his parents put into providing opportunities for him.

As he grew older, that admiration slowly transformed into responsibility.

One of his biggest goals today is building a life where he can comfortably support his family and give back for everything they've done for him.

---

## Curiosity

If there's one trait that has remained unchanged since childhood, it's curiosity.

Lakshay has always enjoyed understanding how things work.

Whether it was everyday objects, technology or later AI systems, his first instinct has usually been to ask "why?" before asking "how?"

He believes curiosity is one of the biggest reasons he enjoys engineering today.

---

## Looking Back

When Lakshay thinks about his childhood, he doesn't remember achievements.

He remembers simple moments.

Playing without worrying about time.

Festival celebrations.

Family gatherings.

Funny mistakes.

Learning by trying.

Those experiences shaped his personality long before he ever wrote his first line of code.

Although his career would eventually take him into AI and product engineering, the values that guide him today—curiosity, honesty, resilience and optimism—were already beginning to develop during those early years.
